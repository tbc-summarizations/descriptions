Alright, buckle up, because I'm about to drag these football logos through the mud like a desperate midfielder trying to win a penalty. Real Madrid: Oh, look, it's Real Madrid, a team whose logo is as inflated as their egos. A crown? Seriously? You'd think after all those Champions League titles, they'd come up with something more original than a glorified tiara. The interlocking letters look like a toddler's attempt at calligraphy. And let's be honest, half their success is just down to buying every superstar that looks vaguely promising. They're basically the footballing equivalent of a trust fund kid who thinks they earned their wealth. Manchester City: Ah, Man City. A symbol of modern football's soulless pursuit of money and glory. Their logo features a boat, presumably sailing on a sea of oil money. The flower? As dull as their stadium's atmosphere. "1894" and "City", just in case someone is too stupid to know what club this is. Overall, it is a badge of generic corporate blandness. Bayern Munich: Bayern Munich's logo is as exciting as watching paint dry. A circle within a circle, and the blue and white pattern inside, a symbol of boredom. The fact that they keep winning the Bundesliga is more of an indictment of the league than a compliment to them. Basically, it's the visual representation of German efficiency and lack of imagination. Liverpool: Oh great, it's Liverpool. Their logo features a Liver Bird, which looks like a pigeon that's been hitting the gym. "You'll Never Walk Alone"? More like "You'll Never Stop Living in the Past". They're always talking about their history and their "special" atmosphere, as if that's going to score goals. Let's be honest, their success is 50% Jurgen Klopp's charisma, 40% lucky bounces, and 10% actual talent. The flames underneath are probably from all the times they got torched in a match. Paris Saint-Germain: A team more famous for Neymar's haircuts and Mbappe's transfer rumors than actual football. Their logo features the Eiffel Tower, a reminder that Paris has more to offer than just this overhyped team. The fleur-de-lis is just there to make them seem classy, even though their on-field antics are anything but. Seriously, how many years can they dominate a farmers league before people stop pretending they're a serious European force? AS Roma: It's the logo of AS Roma. The design features the iconic Capitoline Wolf suckling Romulus and Remus, the mythical founders of Rome. It's a nice, artistic touch. This logo is not too bad to be honest. A little old but nothing else. Inter Milan: Now it's Inter. Their logo is not bad either. It's nothing special either as the IM letters inside the circle don't resemble anything special. Borussia Dortmund: I can't say much about this. It has the letters BVB and number 09, which is also not very creative. Chelsea: Now it's Chelsea. They have some lion carrying a stick in his hand, which is pretty funny. And there are some balls, too. Bayer Leverkusen: This is the worst one out of all of these. Two lions holding a circle with letters. Looks very dull and generic. There you have it, a brutal takedown of some of football's most overrated logos. I hope I've managed to offend at least a few fans in the process.
