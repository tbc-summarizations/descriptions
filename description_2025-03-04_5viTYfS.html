Alright, buckle up because we're about to incinerate some flags: Russia: Oh look, it's the Russian flag. Three stripes, just like their economy: white representing their surrender flag, blue representing the tears of their citizens as they get drafted into a pointless war, and red representing the blood they spill in Ukraine. It’s literally the "surrender in war" starter pack. It's so boring; it's like a poorly layered cake that nobody wants a slice of. Canada: Ah, Canada, the land of overly polite people and a flag that looks like it was designed by a kindergarten class. Seriously, a maple leaf? How cliché can you get? It's like they're trying too hard to be Canadian, which ironically, makes them even less cool. Also, red and white? So basic. It's the flag equivalent of saying "eh" after every sentence. China: Red with some stars? Groundbreaking. It’s the flag that screams, "We're communist, but we're also trying to be a global superpower." Good luck with that, Xi. Also, those stars look like they were haphazardly thrown on there. It looks like someone spilled their golden glitter collection on a cheap red tablecloth and called it a day. It's as subtle as a bullhorn in a library. USA: Stars and stripes forever… boring. Seriously, how many times have we seen this thing? It's like the national anthem – overplayed and underappreciated. The blue corner is like a tiny island of confusion in a sea of red and white stripes. It's the flag of freedom, capitalism, and crippling debt. It's also the flag most likely to be found on a pair of novelty socks. Brazil: Oh, Brazil. You have a diamond inside a rectangle inside another rectangle, and you dare call that design? You even put the word "Progress" on it even though the current state of Brazil is far from it. Seriously, it's like a 1970s album cover threw up on a piece of cloth. Also, let's be honest, the only thing most people know about Brazil is soccer and rainforests. Australia: I've never understood Australia's flag; they have an English flag on the corner of their own flag. It's like they couldn't let go of being colonized. Oh and they also have random stars so people know that it's Australia. It's like a toddler's sticker collection gone wrong. India: This is the most forgettable flag I've ever seen, with orange, white, and green and a wheel in the middle. I would not be able to pick it out of a line-up. India's flag screams "We're spiritual, but also heavily populated." It's like a sunset over a landfill. Argentina: This country looks like it was trying to rip off the sky but failed so bad. The sun in the middle looks like someone's failed art project. It's like a child's drawing of the sun after one too many juice boxes. At least the sky can provide beauty and isn't as useless as this flag. Kazakhstan: This flag is just a random collection of lines and colors that nobody understands. It's the flag that screams, "We're an oil-rich nation with a dictator running the show!" The bird in the center looks like it's trying to fly away from the flag itself. Algeria: This flag is split in half and the moon is on the flag, which can symbolize that the country is still not fully there yet. The flag can easily be confused with other Middle Eastern countries. It's like a poorly executed yin-yang symbol with extra steps. Also, green and white? How original. There you go! Hopefully, you enjoyed this roast of flags as much as I did. What a terrible bunch.
