Alright, here's my brutally honest rating of these football logos, from 1 to 10, based purely on aesthetics and design impact: • S.S. Lazio: 6/10. The eagle is kinda cool, but the shield is boring as hell. The color palette is soft which is nice, but the overall design doesn't scream "powerhouse". • Athletic Club Bilbao: 8/10. This one's intriguing. It is pretty complex and the design is kinda old school, but I do not care for the X's. The mix of the bridge, wolves, and stripes is a bit much. • Manchester United: 7/10. It's iconic, but kinda outdated. The devil is cheesy. Still very recognizable but could do with a modern refresh. • Tottenham Hotspur: 9/10. Sleek and simple, but it gets the job done. The navy-on-white color scheme is clean, and the cock is interesting. Overall, it's a decent modern logo. • Eintracht Frankfurt: 8/10. Love the eagle, but the badge is too basic. The color palette is nice, but the rest of the design is pretty boring. • Olympique Lyonnais: 6/10. The "OL" design is basic, but the gold lion is nice. It's not memorable or exciting, just kinda there. • Olympiacos: 7/10. I kinda like the olive leaves. It's a nice representation of the city and the club. The stars are too much though and clutter the design. • Rangers: 5/10. The lion is cool, but the circle and font are lazy. This looks like a generic stock logo. • Bodø/Glimt: 2/10. What the hell is this? It's a messy, ugly, circular atrocity. • Anderlecht: 7/10. It has a classic vibe. The colors are okay, but the design doesn't stand out too much.
